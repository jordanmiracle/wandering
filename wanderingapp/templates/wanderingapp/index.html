Celebration and Ceremony in the North Cascades Foothills

Wandering Waters offers stunning mountain views, seclusion, and in the distance the sound of the North Fork Nooksack flowing towards the Salish Sea. The venue sits at the base of a forested slope of tall cedar and fir trees. Southward the landscape is defined by the broadly spreading shoulders of Slide Mountain, and towards the east rises Church and Bald Mountains. Maple Creek, a tributary to the Nooksack, quietly meanders through the property, and is home to an abundance of wildlife including all five species of Pacific salmon.

In a spirit of Stewardship, development of Wandering Waters began more than twenty years ago, and was inspired by a vision to create an outdoor space for people to gather and celebrate while preserving and enhancing the habitat for the wildlife whose space we share and whose existence we cherish. The structures are crafted in a rustic style from trees that grew on site, and are thoughtfully arranged to enhance the connections between family, friends, and nature.
